**FACEBOOK GROUP POST**

One thing most Oncor customers don't realize about solar:

When your panels produce more power than you're using, that extra electricity flows back to the grid — and Oncor credits you for it on your next bill.

This is called net metering. It's how solar owners in our area end up with near-zero monthly bills during spring and fall, when production is high and demand is lower.

There are rules around it — how many kilowatts you can export, how the credit rate works — and Oncor's policies differ from other Texas utilities.

We wrote a plain-language guide covering how it works specifically in our territory. Link below if you want to read it without wading through utility jargon.
