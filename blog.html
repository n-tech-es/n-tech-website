Quick Solar Tips for North Texas Homeowners

Tip 1: Check Your REP Before Installing

In Texas's deregulated market, your Retail Electric Provider determines how much you get paid for excess solar energy. Not all plans offer solar buyback — switching to the right plan before your system goes live can add hundreds of dollars to your annual savings.

Tip 2: Claim the 30% Tax Credit

The federal Investment Tax Credit reduces your solar system cost by 30%, dollar for dollar off your federal tax bill. It is available through 2032 and applies to both panels and battery storage. Make sure your tax preparer knows about this credit when you file.

Tip 3: Size Your System for 100% Offset

A properly sized solar system can offset 90–100% of your annual electricity usage. Don't let a company upsell you on a system larger than you need. Pull 12 months of past electric bills and use that number to size your system accurately.

Tip 4: Consider Battery Storage Early

After the 2021 Texas winter storm, thousands of homeowners wished they had battery backup. Adding a battery at the same time as your solar installation is more cost-effective than adding one later, and the tax credit applies to batteries installed alongside solar.
