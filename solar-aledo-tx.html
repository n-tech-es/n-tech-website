Why Aledo Homeowners Are Going Solar

Aledo, Texas has become one of the most desirable communities in the entire DFW region, anchored by its consistently top-ranked Aledo ISD and its position just off I-20 west of Fort Worth. Oncor Electric Delivery serves the Aledo area, and as the community continues to grow at a remarkable pace, the argument for solar grows with it. Homeowners who install solar now lock in a predictable, low cost of energy for the next 25+ years, insulating themselves from whatever Oncor's rate schedule looks like a decade from now.

The federal 30% solar investment tax credit expired December 31, 2025 and is no longer available. Aledo homeowners can still take advantage of two Texas-specific incentives: a sales tax exemption on solar equipment (~8.25%, saving $1,500–$2,000 on a typical system) and a homestead property tax exemption on any home value solar adds. Combined with long-term Oncor bill reductions, solar in Aledo can still make strong financial sense — particularly for larger homes with higher energy usage.

Newer Aledo homes also tend to have features that make solar installations cleaner and more efficient: modern roof structures, proper ventilation, and in many cases architectural designs that include south-facing roof planes ideal for panel placement. If your home was built in the last decade, it is very likely an excellent solar candidate with minimal installation complications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar worth it in Aledo, TX?

Yes — Aledo averages around 229 sunny days per year, and newer homes here are often ideally configured for solar. With Oncor Electric Delivery rates rising and Texas solar tax exemptions available, most Aledo homeowners see payback within 7–10 years.

How does Oncor net metering work for Aledo homeowners?

Oncor's net metering credits your account for excess solar energy sent back to the grid, offsetting your bill during evenings and overcast days. N-Tech manages Oncor interconnection for every Aledo customer.

How much does solar cost in Aledo, TX?

N-Tech prices solar starting at $2.40 per watt in Aledo — fully transparent with no hidden fees. Systems run $18,000–$28,000 before Texas solar tax exemptions. After the credit, your effective cost drops by nearly a third.

Are there solar incentives for Aledo homeowners?

The federal 30% investment tax credit expired December 31, 2025. Texas still offers a sales tax exemption on solar equipment and a property tax exemption on solar-added home value. Oncor net metering provides ongoing monthly savings on top of those.
