How Much Are Solar Panels in Texas? (2026 Pricing Guide)

In 2026, solar panels in Texas cost between $2.40 and $3.00 per watt installed. We break down the full numbers — equipment, installation, and what North Texas homeowners actually pay after Texas's remaining solar incentives (the federal ITC expired December 31, 2025).

Home > Blog > How Much Are Solar Panels in Texas?

Investing in solar energy is one of the smartest financial decisions a homeowner in North Texas can make, but the upfront price tag can be confusing. With varying equipment quality, installer rates, and financing options, how do you know if you're getting a fair deal?

Quick Answer: How Much Are Solar Panels in TX?

Solar panels in Texas cost $2.40–$3.00 per watt installed in 2026. For the average North Texas home (10kW system), that's $24,000–$30,000 before incentives and roughly $22,000–$27,500 after Texas's sales tax exemption on equipment (the federal ITC expired Dec 31, 2025). N-Tech starts at $2.40/watt — below the state average.

Texas Solar Incentives in 2026

The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) — which previously reduced solar costs by 30% — expired December 31, 2025 and is no longer available. The incentives that remain for Texas homeowners are state-level:

  • Texas solar equipment sales tax exemption: Solar panels, inverters, and related equipment are exempt from Texas state sales tax (8.25%), saving roughly $1,500–$2,000 on a typical residential system.
  • Texas homestead property tax exemption: The added home value from a solar installation is exempt from property tax reassessment. At North Texas property tax rates of 2–2.5%, this saves $300–$500 per year on a home that gains $15,000–$20,000 in appraised value from solar.

These are real incentives — but smaller than the ITC was. Any quote or savings estimate you received before 2026 that included the ITC needs to be recalculated.

Pro Tip: Buyback Plans

In Texas, choosing the right electricity provider is just as important as the system cost. Look for "Solar Buyback" plans from providers like Rhythm, Almika, or Pulse that offer 1:1 net metering credits for the excess energy your system sends back to the grid.

Equipment Matters: Good vs. Cheap

Not all solar panels are created equal. A "cheap" quote might use older technology with lower efficiency ratings (17–18%). At N-Tech, we recommend Tier-1 panels with roughly 21–23% efficiency.

  • Monocrystalline Panels: Higher efficiency, better aesthetics (all black), slightly higher cost.
  • Microinverters (Enphase): Often preferred over string inverters — if one panel is shaded, the others keep working perfectly.
  • Battery Storage: Adding a Tesla Powerwall or Enphase battery adds $10k–$15k to the cost but provides blackout protection during Texas storms.

Return on Investment (ROI)

With electricity rates in North Texas averaging 14–16 cents per kWh and rising, the payback period for a cash purchase is typically 7 to 10 years. Since solar panels are warranted for 25–30 years, that leaves you with 20+ years of virtually free electricity.

The Bottom Line

Going solar in North Texas isn't just about being green — it's a hedge against inflation. By locking in your energy cost today, you protect yourself from the utility rate hikes that happen every year.

Financing Options for North Texas Homeowners

You don't have to pay for solar out of pocket to make it work financially. N-Tech offers two options — both of which mean you own the system outright from day one:

  • Cash Purchase: Best long-term return on investment. You own the system immediately and your payback period begins right away. The Texas sales tax exemption applies automatically at purchase.
  • Solar Loan: Finance the system over 10–20 years with options including 0% APR for qualified buyers. You still own the system — and many homeowners find their monthly loan payment is close to or less than their current electric bill, even without the ITC.

We don't offer leases or PPAs. With those arrangements, the company owns the panels and the homeowner gets none of the Texas tax exemptions. Our customers own their systems — always.
