Not Sure Solar Is Right Yet? Start With an Energy Audit.

A lot of homeowners come to us with a high electric bill and assume solar is the answer. Sometimes it is. But often there are cheaper fixes — air sealing, insulation, HVAC issues — that should come first. An energy audit tells you exactly what's going on before you spend a dollar on anything.

N-Tech's home energy audit is an in-person visit from a certified professional. We walk through your entire home, document every energy problem we find, and hand you a one-page prioritized action list: fix this first, then this, skip this. Within 48 hours you'll know exactly why your bill is high and what to do about it.

Every audit also includes a free solar readiness assessment — so if solar does make sense for your home, you'll know that too.

Common Questions from North Texas Homeowners

How much does solar cost in Wise County or Parker County?

N-Tech pricing starts at $2.40 per watt, which is below the Texas state average. A typical 10kW system runs around $24,000. The federal 30% tax credit expired December 31, 2025; the Texas solar equipment sales tax exemption (~8.25%) is the primary remaining incentive. Every home is different, so we build a custom quote for each customer based on roof size, energy usage, and sun exposure.

Does solar work well in North Texas weather?

Yes — Wise County, Parker County, Jack County, and Montague County receive an average of 229 sunny days per year, making this region one of the strongest solar markets in the entire country. Even on cloudy days, modern panels generate meaningful power. Hailstorms are a common concern, and all panels we recommend carry impact-resistance ratings appropriate for North Texas conditions.
