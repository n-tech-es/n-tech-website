What to Look for When Comparing Solar Companies in North Texas

When you're evaluating solar installation companies, the per-watt price is the right number to compare — not the monthly payment or the "total system value." A lower per-watt price means a smaller system cost, a shorter payback period, and a larger slice of the 30% federal tax credit going back to you. N-Tech starts at $2.40/watt. The Texas state average for solar installation sits around $3.00–$3.50/watt. That gap is real and it compounds.

The second thing worth asking any solar installation company is whether they use in-house crews or subcontractors — and whether the person quoting your job is a commissioned salesperson. N-Tech doesn't knock on doors, doesn't run a call center, and doesn't pay anyone a commission to close your deal. That cost structure is how we keep per-watt pricing below the market average without cutting corners on equipment or workmanship.

We also don't do leases or PPAs. With those arrangements, the solar company keeps the federal tax credit and ownership of the panels. N-Tech only offers cash purchases and solar loans — both of which mean you own the system from day one, claim the full 30% credit yourself, and have no complications if you sell your home.

If you're comparing solar companies in Wise County, Parker County, or anywhere in the North Texas area, we're happy to give you a real number — not a range, not a "starting from." Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar worth it in North Texas?

Yes — North Texas averages around 229 sunny days per year, above the national average of 205, making it one of the better solar markets in the country. With Oncor rates rising steadily and the 30% federal tax credit available, most North Texas homeowners see payback within 7–10 years.

Does N-Tech Energy Solutions serve my area in North Texas?

N-Tech serves roughly a 60-mile radius from our home base in Chico, TX — covering most of Wise County, Parker County, Jack County, Montague County, and surrounding areas. Just reach out and we will confirm whether your address falls within our service area.

How does net metering work in North Texas?

Most North Texas homeowners are served by Oncor Electric Delivery, which credits solar customers for excess electricity sent back to the grid. Rural customers served by Tri-County Electric Cooperative (TCEC) or other co-ops have similar programs. N-Tech handles interconnection paperwork regardless of your provider.

How much does solar cost in North Texas?

N-Tech prices solar starting at $2.40 per watt across our entire North Texas service area — transparent, all-in pricing with no hidden fees. After the 30% federal tax credit, most systems run $10,500–$17,500 in effective cost. A free consultation gives you a site-specific number.

What is the federal solar tax credit and does it apply in Texas?

The federal solar investment tax credit (ITC) lets homeowners claim 30% of the system installed cost as a credit on federal income taxes. It applies to all residential solar installations in Texas with no income cap. Unused credit can roll over to subsequent tax years.
