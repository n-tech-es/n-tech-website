Solar in Chico — From Your Neighbors at N-Tech

Chico, Texas is a small rural community along the TX-101 corridor in Wise County — and it's the place N-Tech Energy Solutions calls home. We didn't come here from out of state or set up shop in a distant city suburb. We're part of this community, and that means we understand the homes here: the metal roofs, the wide open lots, the rural electric cooperative billing structure, and the real impact that energy costs have on family budgets in a community like Chico. With parts of the area served by Tri-County Electric Cooperative (TCEC) and others by Oncor, we're fluent in both utility systems and can guide you through the interconnection process no matter who your provider is.

When you go solar with N-Tech in Chico, you're not dealing with a national company running through a call center. You're working with a neighbor who shows up on time, gives you honest answers, and stands behind the work. Our systems start at $2.40/watt with transparent per-watt pricing — no padded equipment costs, no sales commissions layered into your quote. If you've been watching your electric bills climb and wondering whether your property is a good fit for solar, the answer is almost certainly yes — and we're ready to walk you through exactly what it would look like.

For Chico homeowners served by Tri-County Electric Cooperative, net metering is worth understanding. TCEC interconnection allows solar customers to send excess power back to the grid and receive credits on their bill — meaning energy produced on sunny days offsets your usage in the evenings. The federal 30% solar tax credit applies here too, reducing your installed cost by nearly a third in the year you install. The combination typically brings payback to within 8–10 years for most Chico properties.

We have worked on properties throughout the TX-101 corridor and know what to expect: metal roofs are common and work beautifully with solar, lots tend to be spacious enough for larger system sizes, and the open landscape means shading is rarely a major obstacle. If you have been hesitant because you were not sure whether your rural property qualifies, it almost certainly does — and the consultation is free.

Starting is simple. Submit your information through our online consultation form and we will review your utility bills and roof layout. Because we are local, we can often answer questions specific to your road or subdivision that a national installer could not. When you go solar in Chico with N-Tech, you are working with your actual neighbors — and that means we are accountable to you in a way that a company headquartered across the country never could be.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar worth it in Chico, TX?

Yes — Chico averages around 229 sunny days per year and sits in one of the more productive solar regions of North Texas. Net metering through Tri-County Electric Cooperative (TCEC) and the 30% federal tax credit make the financial case even stronger for Chico homeowners.

How does net metering work with Tri-County Electric Cooperative?

Tri-County Electric Cooperative (TCEC) allows solar customers to interconnect with the grid and receive bill credits for excess power generated. N-Tech handles the TCEC interconnection application for Chico homeowners — we know the co-op process well.

Can I go solar if I am served by Tri-County Electric in Chico?

Yes — N-Tech is experienced with TCEC interconnection and has helped Chico-area homeowners navigate the co-op solar process. Rural co-op interconnection works differently than Oncor, and our local familiarity with TCEC means fewer delays.

How much does solar cost in Chico, TX?

N-Tech prices solar starting at $2.40 per watt in Chico — the same transparent pricing across our entire service area. After the federal 30% tax credit, a typical system comes down to an effective cost of roughly $12,600–$15,400.
