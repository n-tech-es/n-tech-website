Why Azle Homeowners Are Going Solar

Azle occupies a unique position along TX-199 and US-730, straddling the line between Parker and Tarrant counties and drawing a mix of suburban and semi-rural residents who value space, community character, and proximity to the outdoors — including nearby Eagle Mountain Lake. That mix of home types actually makes Azle one of the most interesting solar markets in North Texas. Whether you have a newer suburban home with a clear south-facing roofline or an older property on a larger lot, there's almost always a viable solar configuration. And with Oncor Electric Delivery serving the area, the interconnection process is one we know extremely well.

N-Tech Energy Solutions serves Azle residents from both the Parker County and Wise County sides of our service area. We treat every home as a unique design problem — analyzing shading, roof pitch, available square footage, and actual energy consumption before making any recommendation. Our goal is to right-size every system so that your payback period is as short as possible and your savings as real as possible. If you're in Azle and your summer electric bill has been giving you heartburn, a 15-minute consultation with us is worth your time.

The federal 30% solar investment tax credit applies to Azle homeowners just as it does everywhere else, and it is a significant financial lever. For a typical system, it means thousands of dollars returned through your federal tax return in the year after installation. When paired with ongoing Oncor bill savings, most Azle properties reach solar payback well within 10 years — with 15 or more additional years of low-cost generation after that.

Eagle Mountain Lake proximity is also worth noting for homeowners considering battery backup alongside solar. Power outages in areas with aging transmission infrastructure can be more frequent, and a battery system paired with solar provides a reliable energy source during grid disruptions. N-Tech can help you evaluate whether battery storage makes sense for your Azle home as part of the same consultation process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar worth it in Azle, TX?

Yes — Azle averages around 229 sunny days per year and sits in one of the stronger solar production zones in North Texas. With Oncor Electric Delivery rate trends and the 30% federal tax credit, most Azle homeowners reach solar payback in 7–10 years.

How does Oncor net metering work for Azle homeowners?

Oncor credits solar customers for excess electricity sent back to the grid, offsetting your bill during non-generating hours. N-Tech handles all Oncor interconnection paperwork as part of the installation process.

Should I add battery backup to my solar system in Azle?

Battery backup is worth considering in Azle given proximity to Eagle Mountain Lake and potential for grid outages in areas with older infrastructure. A battery paired with solar keeps critical loads powered during disruptions. N-Tech can include storage in your consultation.

How much does solar cost in Azle, TX?

Solar starts at $2.40 per watt through N-Tech in Azle — transparent, all-in pricing. After the 30% federal tax credit, most systems run an effective $12,600–$16,800. Your free consultation includes a specific quote for your home.
