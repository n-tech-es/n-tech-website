Why Decatur Homeowners Are Going Solar

Decatur has been the heart of Wise County for well over a century, anchored by its iconic courthouse square on US-81/287 and a community that takes pride in doing things right. That same sensibility applies to energy. With Oncor Electric Delivery serving the area and summer temperatures regularly pushing past 100°F, electric bills in Decatur have become one of the largest household expenses — and they're only trending higher. Solar gives Decatur homeowners a concrete, long-term answer to rising utility rates, with systems that pay for themselves and keep producing clean energy for decades.

N-Tech Energy Solutions is based right here in Wise County, just 10 miles from Lake Bridgeport and well-familiar with the local homes, roof styles, and utility interconnection process. We work directly with Oncor on your behalf to get your system approved and energized as quickly as possible. Whether your home sits on a large lot north of town or in one of Decatur's established neighborhoods near the school district, we'll design a system sized exactly for your needs — with transparent, per-watt pricing and no high-pressure sales tactics.

One factor that strengthens the financial case for Decatur homeowners is the federal solar tax credit. Under current law, homeowners who install a solar system can claim a 30% federal income tax credit on the total installed cost — meaning a $20,000 system effectively costs $14,000 after the credit. Combined with long-term Oncor bill savings, most Decatur systems reach payback within 7–10 years and then continue generating value for another 15–20 years beyond that.

We also understand that not every home is a slam-dunk for solar. Decatur has a mix of roof ages, orientations, and shading conditions — and we will be the first to tell you if your property is not a good fit right now. A consultation with N-Tech costs nothing and comes with no obligation. We show you the numbers, explain the assumptions, and let you decide what makes sense for your family.

When you are ready to explore solar for your Decatur home, the process starts with a simple consultation form. Frequently Asked Questions

Is solar worth it in Decatur, TX?

Yes — Decatur averages around 229 sunny days per year, well above the national average of 205, making it an excellent solar market. Most Decatur homeowners on Oncor Electric Delivery see payback within 7–10 years, and the federal 30% tax credit significantly lowers the upfront cost.

How does net metering work with Oncor in Decatur?

Oncor's net metering program credits your account for excess solar energy sent back to the grid. Those credits offset what you draw at night or on cloudy days, reducing your monthly bill. N-Tech handles all Oncor interconnection paperwork on your behalf.

How much does solar cost in Wise County?

N-Tech prices solar starting at $2.40 per watt in Wise County — all-in with no hidden fees. A typical system runs $15,000–$25,000 before the 30% federal tax credit. After the credit, most homeowners see an effective cost of $10,500–$17,500.

How long does solar installation take in Decatur?

From signed contract to a live system, most Decatur installations take 2–4 weeks. That includes Wise County permitting, Oncor interconnection approval, and 1–2 days of physical installation. We handle all scheduling and paperwork.

Does N-Tech Energy Solutions serve Decatur, TX?

Yes — N-Tech Energy Solutions is based in nearby Chico, TX, right in Wise County. We serve Decatur and surrounding areas with the same local, hands-on approach we bring to every community we work in.
